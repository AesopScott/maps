Completed VA-042: Security automation and orchestration

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: agentic SOC orchestration should be closed-loop, event-driven automation with detection, triage, action agents, workflow orchestrator, case manager, and feedback loop.
- Triage agent: classifies severity, context, decision scope, confidence, and recommended playbooks.
- Playbook engine: templated workflows for incident families, modeled as DAGs with approval gates at critical nodes.
- Approval layer: separates autonomous, auto-approve/rollback, and human-approval tiers.
- Case manager: single source of truth for incident state, synced with ticketing and communications.
- Feedback loop: post-action telemetry updates confidence and playbook tuning.
- Guardrails: reversibility for autonomous actions, blast-radius constraints, approval audit trail, rate limits, circuit breakers, and human escalation for novel/low-confidence threats.
- Eval/proof: replay historical incidents, synthetic scenario injection, staged rollout from triage-only to tier-1 autonomous actions, measure agreement, false positives, rollbacks, MTTR, analyst satisfaction, and autonomous success rate.
- Product recommendation from Claude: build as internal capability first. Branded Agentic SOC product is premature until playbook library matures and customer use cases stabilize.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- Orchestration is where architecture becomes operational risk. The right pattern is not "agents doing SOC work"; it is a policy-governed workflow fabric where triage, playbooks, approval, case state, and rollback are all first-class.
- The case manager must be source of truth. Tickets, chat, dashboards, and agent memory should project from case state, not compete with it.
- Automated response must be action-tiered. Low-risk reversible actions can be autonomous only with rollback and audit. Novel, high-blast-radius, customer-impacting, legal, or destructive actions must escalate.
- This should integrate with VA-043 approval boundaries, VA-045 shared memory, VA-046 decision engine, and VA-048 case management.
