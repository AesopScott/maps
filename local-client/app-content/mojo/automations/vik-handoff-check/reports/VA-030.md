Completed VA-030: Shadow AI discovery and inventory

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: use privacy-first, signals-based discovery rather than content surveillance.
- Network layer: monitor DNS queries, TLS handshakes, known AI service domains, certificate chains, aggregate throughput, source group, destination domain, timestamp, and bytes transferred. Do not inspect prompts/responses by default.
- Endpoint layer: optional/consent-based browser extension, authenticated API-call telemetry, and limited API-key scanning in accessible config/env locations, not broad personal document/email inspection.
- User input: anonymous departmental surveys and incentivized self-reporting to pair signal detection with human ground truth.
- Inventory: classify by service type, risk tier, user scope, and data exposure class.
- Guardrails: zero-content default, content capture only under explicit incident authorization, differential privacy, range reporting, retention windows, access controls, transparency, audit, and legal boundary checks.
- Eval/proof: 30-60 day consented pilot, compare network discovery against anonymous survey ground truth, audit for zero content capture, risk-rank services, and measure detection sensitivity, privacy false positives, and user trust.
- Product recommendation from Claude: build internally and use externally as enabler/services, not immediate branded SaaS product due org-specific signals, liability, and privacy jurisdiction complexity.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- Shadow AI discovery is valuable only if privacy-preserving by design. If discovery over-collects content, it creates a new governance and trust problem while trying to solve an AI governance problem.
- The right control-plane object is an AI Usage Inventory Evidence Record: observed service, source group, confidence, risk tier, data-class inference, policy status, owner, exception status, and review date.
- Discovery should distinguish known approved AI use, unknown use needing owner review, and prohibited use needing escalation. It should not silently shame individual users or turn governance into surveillance.
- This links directly to VA-029 governance. Discovery without approval workflow creates noise; governance without discovery creates blind spots.

Productization recommendation:
