Completed VA-040: Specialized AI security agents

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: classify specialized agents into SOC triage/alert management, threat hunting/detection engineering, incident response/forensics, vulnerability assessment/prioritization, compliance/risk assessment, and malware/binary analysis.
- Shared components: grounded reasoning, sourced evidence, confidence scores, explainable decisions, human approval gates, uncertainty handling, and integrations with SIEM/threat-intel APIs.
- Guardrails: no auto-execution in customer environments for high-consequence actions; false-positive cost modeling; adversarial robustness testing; data retention/residency controls; no training retention without consent; attribution/source transparency; liability and audit logging.
- Eval/proof: independent labeled benchmark per domain, baseline against human/rule/SIEM performance, calibrated confidence, failure-mode catalog, and customer friction pilots.
- Product recommendation from Claude: selectively productize SOC triage and vulnerability prioritization first; keep incident response, malware analysis, compliance/audit, and threat hunting internal or R&D until proof improves.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- Not all security agents are product candidates. The product boundary should follow liability, action authority, evidence quality, repeatability, buyer clarity, and cost-of-error.
- The safest near-term productizable agents are advisory, high-volume, bounded-decision agents: SOC triage, alert enrichment, and vulnerability prioritization.
- High-liability domains should stay internal/research or copilot-only until eval proof is strong: incident response, malware analysis, compliance interpretation, and threat hunting.
- This taxonomy should become a portfolio decision rubric so we do not accidentally brand every useful internal agent as a customer product.

Productization recommendation:
- Recommendation: recommend_make selectively for SOC triage/alert management and vulnerability prioritization; recommend_implement internal capabilities for high-liability domains.
- Why: SOC triage and vuln prioritization have clearer ROI, lower autonomy risk, better benchmarks, and buyer pain. IR, malware, compliance, and threat hunting need expert judgment and stronger proof before external product claims.
- Product path: define two product-candidate lanes: SOC Triage Agent and Vulnerability Prioritization Agent. Define internal R&D lanes for IR, malware, compliance, and threat hunting.
