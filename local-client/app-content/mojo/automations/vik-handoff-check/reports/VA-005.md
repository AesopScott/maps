Completed VA-005: AI SOC triage assistant

Claude research output: an AI SOC triage assistant should use a collaborative tiering model. The agent receives and normalizes alerts, enriches them with context, suggests severity and next steps, then routes to an analyst for validation. The analyst remains the authority for triage outcome, escalation, closure, and action. The agent learns from analyst overrides only through explicit evaluation cycles.

Architecture pattern:
- Alert Reception and Normalization extracts alert type, source system, affected asset, severity hints, and history.
- Enrichment and Correlation adds asset criticality, related alerts, known playbooks, and pattern matches.
- Analyst Validation Loop shows full reasoning and lets the analyst accept, modify, override, escalate, or close.
- Feedback Loop records agent suggestion, analyst decision, delta, and later uses that data for evaluated improvements.
- The agent never silently suppresses, closes, or executes action from an alert.

Guardrails:
- No auto-closure.
- No silent filtering.
- Explain every severity or priority suggestion.
- No containment, account action, or system change execution.
- Analyst override is authoritative.
- Suppression requires explicit analyst-created rule, not model confidence.
- Audit every suggestion and analyst decision.
- Low confidence must say “unable to categorize” instead of guessing.

Eval suite:
- Triage coverage: percent of alerts where the agent gives a useful suggestion.
- Analyst agreement: percent accepted or modified.
- Confidence calibration against analyst outcomes.
- Time reduction per alert versus analyst-only baseline.
- Missed critical alert rate.
- Alert recall in deprioritized queues.
- Analyst satisfaction and trust.
- Latency from alert arrival to suggestion.
- Regression tests for model drift and stale retraining.
- Integration health checks for SIEM schema/API changes.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-005 is a decision-support interface, not an analyst replacement. Its architecture value is preserving analyst control while reducing context-gathering time. The highest-risk failure is not a bad summary; it is silent downtriage or closure. The control-plane rule is simple: the agent may prepare the triage packet, but the analyst owns the triage decision.

Productization recommendation: internal capability plus enabling component. Do not launch as a standalone SOC triage product yet. Integration complexity, customer-specific alert schemas, compliance restrictions, and liability are too high. Build it first as part of internal SOC/role triage workflows and later embed it inside analyst copilot or SOC orchestration products if pilots show time savings without missed-critical regression.

Boundaries held: no implementation beyond report/state/backlog record, no Git/release, production, external communication, spending, secrets, authority expansion, runtime activation, or promotion.
