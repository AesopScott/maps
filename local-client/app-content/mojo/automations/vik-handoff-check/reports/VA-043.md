Completed VA-043: Human approval and autonomous security operations boundary

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: classify every security operation by reversibility and impact scope. Fully reversible, isolated actions can run autonomously; partially reversible or broader-scope actions require notification, pre-approval, or higher review; irreversible actions require human approval and stronger sign-off.
- Approval matrix: isolated plus fully reversible maps to autonomous; bounded plus fully reversible maps to autonomous with async audit; organization-wide reversible maps to human pre-approval with auto-execute. Partially reversible actions escalate from autonomous with immediate notification to human pre-approval and legal review as scope grows. Irreversible actions require human pre-approval, delay, executive approval, and audit trail depending on scope.
- Guardrails: autonomous rate limits, rollback windows, circuit breakers, staging-first promotion, escalation triggers for unexpected patterns, reviewer identity/2FA, acknowledgement windows, and evidence capture for every decision.
- Eval/proof: seeded incident simulations, zero tolerance for boundary violations in higher-risk tiers, audit comparison against past human decisions, rollback-rate thresholds, and survivor-bias review with the security team.
- Product recommendation from Claude: treat as an internal capability first, not a branded product now. The framework is high-liability, governance-specific, and likely table stakes inside larger platforms; validate internally before deciding whether to productize.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- This boundary matrix should become a core control-plane primitive for every security agent and security product we build. The key is not human-vs-autonomous as a binary; the key is a deterministic risk classifier that can prove why an action is allowed, delayed, escalated, or blocked.
- The classifier should combine action reversibility, blast radius, production/customer impact, data sensitivity, identity/secrets impact, legal/compliance exposure, and confidence/evidence quality.
- Mandatory human approval should remain for destructive, irreversible, production-wide, identity/secrets, customer-impacting, legal/compliance, procurement, external communication, and authority-expanding actions.
- Autonomy can be allowed for low-impact, reversible, time-bounded, pre-approved actions with narrow blast radius, complete evidence capture, and automatic rollback or expiry.
- The approval boundary should be evaluated before execution and again after action planning, because a safe intent can become unsafe when mapped to a concrete tool call.
