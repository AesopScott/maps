Completed VA-010: Autonomous log enrichment and parsing

Claude research output: autonomous log enrichment should use a classification-first pipeline. Raw logs are semantically classified before parsing, then selectively parsed by category, then materialized into memory only with provenance, confidence, and sensitivity controls. The original raw log remains immutable reference; normalized facts without source trace are rejected.

Architecture pattern:
- Semantic Classifier identifies content category, sensitivity tier, and actionable signal.
- Selective Parser applies category-specific extraction templates and records parse confidence.
- Memory Materialization maps parsed content to event, fact, relationship, or exception.
- Provenance chain links raw source to parser rule to materialized memory fact.
- Low-confidence fields remain tentative and cannot drive decisions.

Guardrails:
- Preserve raw source and bidirectional provenance for every memory fact.
- Restricted logs stay out of shared memory unless explicitly approved.
- Redact secrets, tokens, keys, PII, and sensitive identifiers before shared memory materialization.
- Schema mismatch or structural divergence goes to quarantine, not silent adaptation.
- Schema versions prevent downstream interpretation drift.
- Re-parse samples periodically to detect parser instability.
- Memory facts without complete provenance are rejected.

Eval suite:
- Semantic preservation: normalized facts match raw-log meaning under independent review.
- Secret leakage: synthetic secrets never reach final memory.
- Schema stability: parser latency and structural failures stay within thresholds.
- Provenance completeness: every fact traces to source, rule, and confidence.
- Misclassification rate on a hand-labeled corpus.
- Drift tests across old archives and new log sources.
- Redaction adversarial tests.
- Incident register for downstream errors caused by normalization.

The differentiator is memory-aware semantic normalization: logs become safe, cited, confidence-scored operational memory. That is valuable for agentic SOC, compliance, analyst copilot, and exposure products.

Do not launch as generic SaaS log parsing; the defensible wedge is trustworthy log-to-memory transformation.
