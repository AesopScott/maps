Completed VA-001: Autonomous threat hunting

Claude research output: autonomous threat hunting should use a quiet-by-default heartbeat evidence-scoring model. The architecture has three layers: continuous inspection, evidence scoring, and notification gating. Signals are collected asynchronously from approved sources, scored with source credibility, time decay, corroboration, contradiction handling, and operator feedback. Most observations stay silent in an evidence ledger or digest; only high-confidence, corroborated, high-impact findings escalate.

Architecture pattern:
- Continuous inspection layer polls approved sources on a schedule.
- Evidence accumulator records observations with source, timestamp, indicator, and confidence.
- Evidence scoring weights source credibility, temporal freshness, corroboration, contradiction, and prior operator feedback.
- Notification gate routes evidence into silent ledger, digest, async review, or critical escalation.
- Heartbeat digest summarizes low-confidence observations without creating alert noise.
- All scoring decisions are logged with agent/config version for replay.

Guardrails:
- Quiet baseline by default; no notification below threshold unless corroborated.
- Known-benign patterns expire and require periodic review.
- Rate limits prevent operator alert floods.
- Evidence decays so old signals do not accumulate into false urgency.
- Operator false-positive feedback tunes weights but does not erase audit evidence.
- No autonomous containment or response action; the agent inspects, scores, recommends, and routes.
- Source and tenant boundaries must be enforced so unrelated evidence does not bleed across scope.

Eval suite:
- Signal fidelity: real indicator appears quickly and gains confidence when corroborated.
- Noise suppression: known-benign scheduled activity remains below escalation threshold.
- Temporal coherence: stale indicators decay and do not trigger late false alerts.
- Contradiction handling: conflicting signals dampen confidence.
- Digest quality: low-confidence observations are grouped and actionable.
- Operator latency: high-confidence notification arrives quickly.
- Audit trail: any alert can be replayed from evidence and scoring metadata.
- Feedback learning: repeated false-positive patterns score lower after review.
- Multi-source boost: independent corroboration increases confidence.
- Scale resilience: high-volume observation streams do not drop signals or spam operators.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-001 is a notification-control and evidence-correlation pattern. Its main value is not “autonomous hunting” in the heroic sense; it is disciplined signal governance. The agent must prove continuous inspection without converting every weak signal into operational noise. This directly supports Mojo heartbeat/file-watch behavior and future SOC/security products.

Productization recommendation: internal capability first, with a path to enabling component. Do not brand as a standalone threat-hunting product yet. The market is crowded and trust-sensitive. Build internally to calibrate evidence scoring and operator-noise thresholds. If proven, package the scoring/heartbeat layer as an embedded component inside analyst copilot, log enrichment, exposure graph, or SOC orchestration products.
