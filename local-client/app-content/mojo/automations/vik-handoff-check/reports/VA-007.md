Completed VA-007: Automated phishing and social engineering analysis

Claude research output: phishing and social-engineering analysis should use a synchronous classifier with bounded context and segregated outcomes. The agent ingests one message at a time, separates metadata/body/attachments, performs static analysis, emits a risk label and diagnostic signals, and stops. It must not block, delete, forward, reply, update policy, contact senders, or automatically escalate.

Architecture pattern:
- Input Stage separates sender, recipient, headers, body, URLs, and attachments.
- Analysis Engine uses linguistic risk, URL/domain reputation, credential-request patterns, urgency markers, spoofing indicators, and confidence scoring.
- Output Stage returns internal risk classification, confidence, and explanation only.
- Execution Model is synchronous request-response, not ambient monitoring.
- State Boundary is stateless unless human-reviewed feedback is separately approved for model or rule updates.

Guardrails:
- No attachment execution or URL fetching outside approved static reputation feeds.
- Maximum message size and metadata-only mode.
- Classification output only; no action recommendation that bypasses human/security workflow.
- Reasoning must identify signals without exposing unnecessary PII or sensitive content.
- No blocklist, allowlist, mailbox, or policy updates.
- Audit every analysis request with input hash, signal output, confidence, and model/config version.
- Human review required before feedback becomes a new signal.
- Non-English or unsupported-language messages must lower confidence rather than invent certainty.

Eval suite:
- Phishing corpus covering credential harvest, CEO fraud, impersonation, malware delivery, and legitimate enterprise mail.
- True-positive and false-positive measurement by attack class.
- Explanation quality review by security analysts.
- Adversarial samples: homoglyphs, obfuscation, leetspeak, misleading styling, and benign high-urgency mail.
- Privacy/sensitive-context checks for HR, finance, executive, and internal messages.
- Monthly false-positive/false-negative review.
- Quarterly “explain the miss” sessions.
- Live PoC with injected phishing and real benign mail under approval.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-007 is highly relevant to Mindshare communications governance. The correct shape is an internal-message risk classifier and message-risk signal component, not an automated responder. It should detect suspicious requests, authority-bypass attempts, impersonation patterns, urgency pressure, and credential/social-engineering cues while preserving human routing and review.

Productization recommendation: enabling component. Do not ship as a standalone email-security product now. The mature market expects blocking, quarantine, gateway integrations, and policy enforcement. Mojo’s safer wedge is a message-risk classifier embedded into communications governance, SOC triage, analyst copilot, or partner integrations. White-label signal API may be viable after benchmark and live-mail proof.

Boundaries held: no implementation beyond report/state/backlog record, no Git/release, production, external communication, spending, secrets, authority expansion, runtime activation, or promotion.
