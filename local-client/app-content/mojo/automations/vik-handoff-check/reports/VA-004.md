Completed VA-004: Behavioral baseline monitoring

Claude research output: behavioral baseline monitoring should use a layered anomaly framework: behavioral fingerprinting, multi-signal drift detection, violation discrimination, and human review. Each role needs a stable baseline across decision patterns, output semantics, interaction style, and temporal rhythm. Drift should be detected statistically and semantically, then filtered to distinguish normal environmental change or learning from concerning behavioral shifts.

Architecture pattern:
- Behavioral Fingerprinting establishes role baselines across decisions, outputs, style, and pacing.
- Drift Detection compares current behavior against baseline using statistical divergence, semantic distance, structural breaks, and persistence across task contexts.
- Violation Discrimination classifies root cause: environmental change, gradual learning, random jitter, or systematic concerning shift.
- Review and Escalation produces alerts only when anomaly score exceeds threshold and fails benign-change filters.
- Alerts include concrete before/after examples, not only abstract scores.

Guardrails:
- Do not activate drift detection until baseline maturity exists.
- Freeze baseline versions so contaminated history cannot rewrite the past.
- Suppress known benign context shifts when task mix or instructions change.
- Compare against peer-role/cohort shifts to avoid mistaking environment-wide change for individual drift.
- Require persistence across multiple observations before alerting.
- Keep immutable baseline and drift-score audit logs.
- Tune thresholds by role class rather than one global rule.
- Human review remains the verdict; monitoring is an alert trigger only.

Eval suite:
- Baseline stability test across early/late baseline slices.
- False-positive test over normal operation.
- Retroactive validation against known historical drift incidents.
- Discrimination classifier accuracy for benign vs concerning drift.
- Performance overhead measurement.
- Contextual sensitivity test after known instruction/task changes.
- Interpretability check requiring concrete examples in every alert.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-004 is the control-plane equivalent of “agent behavioral integrity.” It should not punish normal learning, new task distribution, or role maturation. It should detect unexplained, persistent, authority-relevant drift: changing decision values, communication boundaries, tool-use posture, escalation behavior, or response shape. This is especially important before any role moves toward persistent autonomy.

Productization recommendation: enabling platform component plus internal capability. Do not launch as a standalone branded security product now. The market education burden is high, and the false-positive risk is operationally serious. Build internally for Vik/Mojo roles, prove baseline stability and useful drift alerts, then embed as part of a future trustworthy-agent or agentic security control-plane product.
