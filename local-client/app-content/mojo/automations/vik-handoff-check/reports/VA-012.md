Completed VA-012: AI security copilot for analysts

Claude research output: a security copilot should use context-gated dual-layer guardrails. The first layer captures authorization context such as defensive use, research, CTF, or approved pentest scope. The second layer classifies requested technique, target scope, and output form. The copilot may reason about adversarial techniques, but it must block unsafe execution, mass targeting, supply-chain compromise, unscoped credential attacks, detection evasion, persistence, and cross-org harm.

Architecture pattern:
- Authorization Context records declared purpose, engagement ID or research brief, target scope, time window, and reviewer/signature where needed.
- Safe-Action Classifier evaluates target scope, technique family, and output form.
- Hard Blocks reject mass targeting, supply-chain compromise, unscoped evasion kits, cross-org exfiltration, and persistent unauthorized access.
- Context-Gated Blocks require verified authorization for credential testing, exploit development, C2 deployment, or lab-only actions.
- Permissive Reasoning allows analysis, education, defensive explanation, and scoped research without granting execution authority.
- Every block, allow, override, and context claim is logged for audit.

Guardrails:
- “I am a pentester” is not enough; authorization context must be verifiable.
- Reasoning is allowed more broadly than execution.
- Output form matters: explanation differs from deployable automation.
- No open-ended targets, mass scanning, supply-chain backdoors, or stealth/evasion kits.
- Overrides require analyst signature and audit trail.
- Eval suite and guardrail version must move together.
- Guardrail reports should show categories and trends without leaking exact bypass tests.

Eval suite:
- Negative tests for mass scanning, supply-chain compromise, evasion generation, cross-scope exfiltration, and unscoped credential attacks.
- Positive tests for safe educational explanation, scoped research, CTF tasks, and verified pentest contexts.
- Borderline-case corpus with human review.
- Quarterly guardrail regression suite with versioned results.
- False-negative audit using real pentesting workflows.
- False-positive audit using legitimate analyst work.
- Governance dashboard tracking blocks, overrides, context mismatches, and eval score trends.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-012 is not just a product feature; it is the contract for every Mojo analyst-facing AI surface. The main architectural move is separating “understand and explain” from “execute and operationalize.” A good copilot can help analysts reason about attack paths without becoming an unbounded offensive tool.

Productization recommendation: enabling component with branded product potential through higher-level offerings. Do not sell guardrails alone as the product. Use this as the safety core inside Mojo Analyst Copilot, SOC triage, malware co-analyst, vulnerability validation, and red-team governance. The branded product should be the analyst copilot or security control-plane product; this guardrail layer is a defensible internal component and licensing asset.

Boundaries held: no implementation beyond report/state/backlog record, no Git/release, production, external communication, spending, secrets, authority expansion, runtime activation, or promotion.
