Completed VA-006: Multi-step incident investigation

Claude research output: multi-step incident investigation should use a hypothesis-driven workflow with explicit scope gates. The agent starts from an investigation charter, collects scoped evidence, forms ranked hypotheses, validates the strongest hypotheses, eliminates alternatives, and closes only with a confidence score and evidence trail. Evidence indexing prevents re-exploration and wandering.

Architecture pattern:
- Investigation Charter defines domain, scope, owner, timebox, and stop conditions.
- Evidence Collection gathers only scoped artifacts and records timestamp, source, and purpose.
- Hypothesis Formation keeps a ranked set of active hypotheses, not an open-ended exploration tree.
- Targeted Validation tests the highest-ranked hypotheses against evidence.
- Root Cause Synthesis requires alternative hypotheses to be tested and eliminated.
- Closure includes confidence, unresolved questions, out-of-scope findings, and human review requirement.

Guardrails:
- Scope boundary rejects unrelated evidence or flags need for a new investigation charter.
- Evidence saturation stops collection once enough independent sources converge.
- Active hypotheses are capped to prevent combinatorial sprawl.
- Iteration budgets prevent token/runaway investigation loops.
- Cycle detection flags repeated artifact reads without a new purpose.
- Every accepted hypothesis needs multiple evidence sources.
- Root-cause closure requires tested alternatives, not just one plausible story.

Eval suite:
- Root-cause accuracy against postmortem ground truth.
- Hypothesis efficiency: correct hypotheses tested versus total considered.
- Scope adherence: evidence gathered stays inside charter.
- Convergence speed within iteration budget.
- False-positive rate on incidents with planted misleading evidence.
- Confidence calibration against known outcomes.
- Reproducibility across repeated runs with same incident data.
- Manual audit of evidence linkage and conclusion support.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-006 is a workflow-control pattern for investigations. The key failure it prevents is wandering: repeated searches, expanding scope, narrative confidence without evidence, and premature closure. For Mojo, the same pattern applies to architecture incidents, role drift investigations, release/gate failures, and security investigations.

Productization recommendation: enabling component, internal first. Do not sell standalone yet. Multi-step investigation needs deep integration with incident data, ticketing, telemetry, and postmortem records. Build the investigation charter/evidence/hypothesis engine internally and embed it later inside analyst copilot, SOC orchestration, or architecture-control products if blind evaluations prove root-cause accuracy and reproducibility.
