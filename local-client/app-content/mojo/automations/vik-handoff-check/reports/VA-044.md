Completed VA-044: Self-healing infrastructure controls

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: use an Observe -> Validate -> Execute -> Verify -> Revert cycle with explicit gates. Detect anomaly, classify impact, generate remediation plan, run dry-run/blast-radius analysis, deploy in staged canaries, validate continuously, and roll back on regression.
- Scope rule: self-healing should only be active for high-confidence, low-risk remediations. Authentication, encryption, multi-service dependency, customer-impacting, and broad production repairs require human approval or escalation.
- Guardrails: blast-radius bounds, canary rollout, automatic rollback thresholds, state-change bookkeeping, cooldown windows, circuit breakers, diff-before-apply checks, post-repair audit, periodic reconciliation, and confidence scoring.
- Loop prevention: block repeated identical actions inside a time window; enforce cooldown after rollback; escalate after repeated reverts instead of retrying forever.
- Drift prevention: compare intended and live state before applying, distinguish ambiguous operator intent from true drift, and alert instead of auto-repairing when intent is unclear.
- Eval/proof: staging simulation, chaos testing, shadow-mode production dry runs, gated pilot by narrow repair category, observability audit, false-positive targets, rollback success proof, and MTTR comparison.
- Product recommendation from Claude: build first as an internal capability, not an immediate branded product. Validate guardrails internally, then consider productization after repeatable evidence.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- This should be modeled as controlled remediation, not broad self-healing. The product/control-plane primitive is a Remediation Governor: it decides whether a proposed repair may be planned, simulated, applied, rolled back, or escalated.
- The key architectural requirement is a durable repair ledger. Every repair needs intent, evidence, affected assets, blast-radius estimate, approved policy, pre-state, post-state, rollback plan, validation result, and human override status.
- Self-healing should be prohibited when the system cannot prove current state, cannot prove rollback, detects competing writers, sees ambiguous operator intent, or crosses production/customer/secrets/identity boundaries.
- The strongest product value is not the repair action itself; it is provable safe automation: simulation, blast-radius prediction, approval gates, rollback, and audit evidence.

Productization recommendation:
- Recommendation: recommend_implement as an enabling component and internal capability; defer standalone branded product.
- Why: automated repair is strategically valuable for every security product we might build, but standalone self-healing security carries high customer risk and needs proof before brand promise.
- Product path: create a reusable Self-Healing Control Pattern and Remediation Governor that can later power branded tools such as autonomous hardening, compliance repair, or incident containment.
