Completed VA-015: Exposure relationship analysis

Claude's research output:
- Model exposure as a directed, multi-type authority graph across identities, systems, permissions, and workflows.
- Use nodes for users, service accounts, agents, groups, resources, APIs, data stores, infrastructure components, scoped permissions, and workflow contexts.
- Use typed edges such as has-permission, requires-permission, accesses, executes-as, and depends-on, with temporal validity, confidence, source provenance, delegation chains, and blast-radius traversal.
- Core agent operations should be authority resolution, exposure mapping from a compromise point, and least-trust path finding for approved workflows.
- Guardrails should prevent agents from inventing relationships, modifying policy edges, or acting on inferred authority without explicit approval. Explicit deny must override implicit allow; low-confidence edges must be labeled as inference; graph queries and result sets must be audit logged.
- Proof should include synthetic authority-resolution accuracy, exposure-path completeness against known attack paths, conflict detection, least-privilege recommendation comparison, and real-world pilots on high-risk systems.

Vik architecture-owner interpretation:
VA-015 should become the authority substrate behind Mojo security agents. The key pattern is not just a graph database; it is a source-bound authority model where every relationship has provenance, confidence, scope, and time. Agents may read and reason over the graph, but policy mutation and containment action remain gated. This gives us a shared way to answer: who can do what, through which workflow, against which system, under which approval contract, and what happens if that identity or workflow is compromised.

Productization recommendation:
Build as an enabling component first, with strong branded-product potential once proven. I would not launch it initially as a standalone branded product called an authority graph; customers buy exposure reduction, identity-risk analysis, and least-privilege proof. The product path is to build it as the Mindshare/Mojo authority graph layer, then package customer-facing modules such as exposure mapper, least-privilege synthesizer, conflict resolver, and blast-radius explorer. Candidate branded product direction: Identity Exposure Graph or Authority Risk Graph after pilots prove low false positives and useful attack-path discovery.
