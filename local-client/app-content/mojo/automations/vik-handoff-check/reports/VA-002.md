Completed VA-002: AI-assisted threat intelligence correlation

Claude research output: AI-assisted threat intelligence correlation should use weighted indicator fusion with source reputation and explicit conflict resolution. Indicators from heterogeneous sources should be normalized into a shared schema, scored by source reputation, freshness, confidence interval, corroboration, and contradiction. Recommendations should include confidence bands, source breakdown, conflict flags, and a clear explanation of why one signal won over another.

Architecture pattern:
- Indicator Registry normalizes IPs, domains, hashes, behavioral signals, network patterns, and role/security observations into one schema.
- Source Reputation Layer tracks source accuracy against verified outcomes and adjusts source weight over time.
- Fusion Logic combines weak indicators without treating one source as authority.
- Conflict Resolution uses reputation first, recency second, confidence interval third, and human review when the margin is narrow.
- Recommendation Output includes confidence, uncertainty, source contributions, conflict notes, and action boundary.

Guardrails:
- No single source should dominate high-stakes recommendations without corroboration.
- New or unproven sources need enough historical verdicts before receiving high trust.
- Stale indicators must decay or be discarded by domain-specific TTL.
- Every recommendation must be explainable by source, weight, recency, and conflict path.
- Ground truth feedback must adjust source reputation without erasing prior audit evidence.
- Weak signals alone should not trigger urgent recommendations; require multiple independent weak signals or at least one stronger signal.

Eval suite:
- Historical precision and recall against verified incident corpus.
- Conflict-resolution accuracy when sources disagree.
- Source-reputation calibration against actual error rate.
- Analyst acceptance rate during pilot.
- Time-to-validation for recommendations.
- Adversarial source-injection tests.
- Source-outage degradation tests.
- Conflicting-data flood tests.
- Explainability audit by analysts who did not build the model.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-002 is a trust-fusion pattern. It is useful wherever Mojo needs to combine weak, partial, or conflicting evidence: threat intel feeds, role memory, handoff channels, release decisions, Obsidian notes, repo state, and future security product telemetry. The key rule is source pluralism without authority confusion: no source becomes canonical by accident, and every recommendation exposes how evidence was weighted.

Productization recommendation: enabling component. Do not sell as a standalone threat-intelligence product now. Customers buy action-ready security context, not generic fusion math. Build internally first, prove with a verified ground-truth corpus and analyst pilot, then embed as a scoring API or module inside analyst copilot, log enrichment, exposure graph, or SOC orchestration products.
