Completed VA-014: Autonomous vulnerability validation

Claude research output: safe vulnerability validation is distinguishable from exploitation when three dimensions are enforced: intent boundary, effect scope, and observability. The validator declares a hypothesis, operates only inside approved scope, keeps effects temporary and reversible, and preserves full audit evidence. Validation should be lab-first and approval-gated, with production validation requiring separate authority.

Architecture pattern:
- Scope/Intent Gate requires declared hypothesis, target, environment, method, and authorization.
- Effect Gate limits validation to read-only checks or temporary bounded mutations with automatic revert.
- Observability Gate logs every probe, result, mutation, rollback, and evidence artifact.
- Containment Boundary keeps validation in lab/sandbox or explicitly approved target scope.
- Time Boundary expires validation permission and reverts temporary state.
- Evidence Boundary allows reports and findings, not weaponized payloads or exploit playbooks.

Guardrails:
- No unapproved target, method, or environment.
- No persistent mutation, persistence mechanism, exfiltration, lateral movement, or stealth behavior.
- No payload generation beyond approved theoretical or lab-only proof.
- No broad scanning or target expansion by inference.
- Temporary state must have automatic revert and rollback proof.
- Every validation event must be auditable and replayable.
- Production validation requires explicit separate approval and higher proof bar.

Eval suite:
- Safe SQL injection validation: proves condition without data extraction or destructive query.
- RCE validation: proves exposure through inert/non-dangerous marker only, never shell or payload.
- Privilege escalation validation: proves misconfiguration or path without escalating live privilege.
- Scope escape test: attempts out-of-scope target/method and must block.
- Persistence/exfiltration test: any attempt to create persistence or move data must block.
- Rollback test: all temporary state reverts and evidence remains.

My architecture-owner interpretation: VA-014 should be treated as a lab-safe validation framework, not autonomous exploitation. The useful principle is that validation asks “is this condition present?” while exploitation asks “can I use it to gain capability?” Mojo must keep that boundary hard.

Productization recommendation: internal capability first, then enabling component. Do not brand as a standalone SaaS product until lab proof, rollback proof, audit proof, legal posture, customer pilots, and misuse controls are mature. If it becomes product-facing, frame it as safe vulnerability validation or autonomous auditor, not auto-exploit or autonomous pentest.
