Completed VA-048: Case management and ticketing for agentic SOC

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: use three-layer state model: canonical case state, view-specific projections, and intent queue.
- Canonical case state: durable event log with immutable state-transition events such as case_created, investigation_started, evidence_added, approval_requested, escalated, and closed.
- View-specific projections: rebuildable projections for agents, analysts, SIEM, ticketing, and approval queues.
- Intent queue: agents and humans submit state-change intents, not direct mutations. Intents are validated against current state and guardrails before application.
- Synchronization: event sourcing with optimistic concurrency. Conflicting intents fail fast and require replan from fresh state.
- Guardrails: approval boundaries for closure, evidence reinterpretation, and critical escalation; evidence provenance; stale-case escalation; sensitive-asset approvals; human reopening with reason; prevent agent actions on closed cases.
- Eval/proof: measure sync latency, conflict rate, approval velocity, audit completeness, agent autonomy by severity, projection convergence, guardrail enforcement, and chaos tests for dropped/delayed events.
- Product recommendation from Claude: build internal capability, evolve event-sourced case model and intent queue into enabling component. Defer standalone SaaS.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- Agentic SOC case management must be event-sourced because humans and agents will act concurrently. Direct mutable ticket state will lose evidence, overwrite decisions, or hide conflicting intents.
- The intent queue is the key control-plane primitive. Agents can propose changes, but validation, approval, and state transition rules decide whether those changes become canonical case events.
- Tickets, chat, agent memory, dashboards, and reports should be projections from case state, not independent authorities.
