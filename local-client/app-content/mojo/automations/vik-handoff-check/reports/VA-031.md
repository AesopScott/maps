Completed VA-031: AI risk register and compliance evidence

Claude's research output:
- Architecture: represent risk as a versioned entity with immutable evidence chains.
- Risk record: id, timestamps, version, threat category, severity and justification, AI asset, owner, deployment context, control state, and evidence chain.
- Evidence log entry: timestamp, actor, action type, artifact reference, assertion, confidence, and expiry/re-evaluation date.
- Incident record: trigger, realized risk, response timeline, remediation evidence, risk adjustment, and rationale.
- Version awareness: immutable evidence logs, frozen risk snapshot at approval time, incident-to-risk links, and retraction-with-reason instead of deletion.
- Guardrails: approval authority matrix, required artifact-backed evidence, stale-risk detection, incident-triggered escalation, segregation of duties, and data minimization.
- Eval/proof: simulate model deployment lifecycle, block deployment on evidence gaps, map fields to NIST AI RMF/audit queries, replay incidents from evidence logs, define schema JSON and workflow state machine, and create audit query templates.
- Product recommendation from Claude: build as internal capability now, not standalone branded product. Use lightweight schema and audit log first, then expand to incident ingestion and compliance reporting.

Vik's architecture-owner interpretation:
- AI risk register is the connective tissue between governance, release, runtime observation, incidents, and product assurance. Without versioned evidence, safety claims become narrative instead of proof.
- The register should not be a document graveyard. It should be an evidence-indexed state machine: planned -> implemented -> tested -> approved -> monitored -> stale/reopened.
- Strongest primitive: Risk Evidence Ledger. It should link model/version, agent/runtime, policy, control, approval, eval result, incident, exception, and owner route.
- Data minimization matters. Store evidence references and aggregate proof, not raw sensitive payloads or user data.
